# Google Cybersecurity Certificate – Course 2: Play It Safe: Manage Security Risks

I recently completed **Course 2** of the Google Cybersecurity Certificate, *Play It Safe: Manage Security Risks*. This course focused on understanding how organizations identify, assess, and reduce cybersecurity risks while building a strong security foundation.

Throughout the course, I learned about the **nine CISSP security domains** and how they relate to different areas of information security. I also gained a better understanding of the differences between **threats, vulnerabilities, and risks**, and how security professionals evaluate and manage them.

The course introduced different types of **security controls** (administrative, technical, and physical) and explored widely used **security frameworks**, including the **NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)**. I learned how the RMF helps organizations manage cybersecurity risks through a structured, continuous process.

In addition, I explored **Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)** tools and how they collect and analyze security logs to detect suspicious activity. I also learned about **incident response playbooks**, which provide standardized procedures for responding to common security incidents efficiently and consistently.

This course strengthened my understanding of cybersecurity risk management and provided practical knowledge that will help me as I continue learning and developing my skills in cybersecurity.
