 Google Cybersecurity Certificate – Course 1: Foundations of Cybersecurity

# Overview
This course introduces the cybersecurity field: what the job actually involves, the
mindset needed, and the foundational frameworks used across the industry.

 Key Concepts

 What Security Analysts Actually Do
- Monitor systems for suspicious activity
- Respond to and investigate security incidents
- Use tools like SIEMs to detect threats
- Protect organizations from unauthorized access and data breaches

 CIA Triad (revisited in more depth)
- **Confidentiality** – only authorized people can access data
- **Integrity** – data is accurate and hasn't been tampered with
- **Availability** – systems/data are accessible when needed

 Common Threats & Attack Types
- Social engineering (phishing, pretexting)
- Malware (viruses, ransomware, spyware)
- Insider threats
- Basic overview of how breaches typically happen

 Security Frameworks & Compliance
- Introduction to frameworks like NIST
- Why compliance and standardized frameworks matter for organizations

The 8 CISSP Security Domains
Introduced as a map of the broader security field — not deep detail yet, but useful for
understanding how different specialties (network security, risk management, etc.) fit
together.

 What I Learned
This course was more conceptual/foundational than technical — it gave me the vocabulary
and big-picture view of the field before getting hands-on. Pairs well with what I already
covered in TryHackMe's Pre Security path, since a lot of terminology overlapped.
